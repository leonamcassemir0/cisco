1.2 Data is All Around Us
DISCRETE DATA is data collected by counting. 
	- It is whole, concrete numbers. 
	- Discrete data typically involves counting rather 		than measuring and is often prefixed with “the 	number of”.
CONTINUOUS DATA is data collected by measuring.

1.2.3 Data Types
STRING is a text.
INTEGER is a whole number.
FLOATING numbers with decimal places.
DATE AND TIME.
BOOLEAN true(yes or 1) and false(no or 0).

1.2.6 Structured and Unstructured Data
STRUCTURED DATA is organized and searchable.
	- Used with rows and columns.
UNSTRUCTURED DATA is more diverse and challenging to work with.
	- Examples: photos, videos, social media posts, emails, 	and other variable content.
